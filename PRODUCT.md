# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers at robotics, AI, and industrial-tech companies (Siemens-class engineering orgs, robotics startups, applied-ML teams). They arrive from a CV link or LinkedIn, skim in under two minutes, and decide whether Sebastian gets an interview. Secondary: engineers on the hiring panel who click through to verify depth.

## Product Purpose

A single-page portfolio for Sebastian Barrio Bejarano, AI engineer with a mechatronics foundation (Siemens AI research intern, autonomous mobile robots, computer vision, LLM workflows). It must convert a skim into an interview request: prove technical depth fast, show the actual systems he built (robotics planning, SLAM, YOLO vision, IK solvers), and make contact effortless. Success = recruiter reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Precise, inky, engineered. The site reads like high-grade technical print: engineering documentation elevated to editorial art. Dithered halftone graphics rendered from his actual domains (occupancy grids, flow fields, kinematic linkages) carry the visual identity. Calm confidence, zero hype; the work speaks in specifics (98% accuracy, 4-DoF, ML-RRT) rather than adjectives.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev-portfolio template: avatar circle, "Hi, I'm X 👋", skill-bar percentages, identical project cards with GitHub icons.
- Corporate SaaS landing page: gradient blobs, glassmorphism, hero metrics, purple-on-white startup grammar.
- Over-animated showpiece: scroll-jacking, multi-second intro loaders, motion that delays the content.
- Dark "hacker" portfolio: terminal green-on-black, matrix rain, neon glow.

## Design Principles

1. **Print, not screen.** Every surface behaves like ink on bright paper: dithered imagery, hairline rules, typographic confidence. If an element wouldn't survive a risograph pass, it doesn't belong.
2. **The work is the imagery.** Visuals are procedurally drawn from Sebastian's real domains (SLAM maps, configuration-space densities, robot linkages), never decorative stock.
3. **Specifics convert.** Numbers, model names, and algorithms over adjectives. A recruiter should be able to quote the site in a Slack message to the team.
4. **Two-minute skim, ten-minute depth.** Hierarchy serves the skimmer; detail rewards the engineer who stays.
5. **Motion is mechanical.** Animation moves like instrumentation: precise, damped, purposeful. Nothing bounces.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 on paper background. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives (animated dither fields render a static frame). Semantic landmarks and heading order for screen readers; all canvas graphics get descriptive labels. Keyboard-reachable contact actions.
